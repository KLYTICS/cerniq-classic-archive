## 1. Who We Are

CERNIQ is an institutional risk intelligence platform built specifically for Puerto Rico cooperativas and U.S. credit unions. We generate bilingual (Spanish + English) ALM reports that are COSSEC-aligned, board-ready, and delivered in hours instead of weeks.

We are not a fintech startup. We are not an AI company. We are a reporting and compliance tool that automates what consultants charge $8,000-$12,000 per quarter to do manually.

**Company:** KLYTICS LLC, San Juan, Puerto Rico
**Product:** CERNIQ -- plataforma de analisis ALM
**Domain:** cerniq.io

---

## 2. Who Speaks

**Erwin Kiess, Fundador.** Every client-facing email, every follow-up, every report review session comes from Erwin personally. The voice is that of a senior risk analyst who has sat in ALCO meetings, reviewed COSSEC examination reports, and understands the pressure a cooperativa CFO faces when exam season arrives.

**Tone qualities:**
- **Specific** -- We cite ratios, basis points, delivery timelines, and page counts. Never vague.
- **Data-driven** -- We say "12 ratios regulatorios" and "1,000 escenarios Monte Carlo", not "comprehensive analysis."
- **Bilingual** -- Spanish is the primary language for cooperativa communication. English accompanies it, never leads it.
- **Direct** -- Short sentences. No hedging. "Su informe esta listo" not "We're pleased to inform you that your report has been generated."
- **Personal** -- "Respondame directamente" not "Contact our support team." Erwin answers every email.

## 3. Our Audience

**Primary: Cooperativa CFOs and Treasurers**
- Manage $50M-$500M+ in assets
- Report to COSSEC and their board of directors
- Prepare materials for ALCO committee meetings quarterly
- Under pressure to demonstrate regulatory compliance
- Spanish-dominant in daily work; comfortable in English for technical terms
- Value reliability and specificity over novelty

**Secondary: ALCO Committee Members**
- Board members reviewing ALM risk reports
- Need executive summaries, not raw data
- Often non-technical; need clear risk language (Fuerte / Moderado / En riesgo)

**Tertiary: CPA Firms and Consultants**
- Serve multiple cooperativas simultaneously
- Need multi-client workflow and consolidated views
- English-primary but serve Spanish-speaking institutions
- Care about margins, deliverable quality, and client retention

**Who we are NOT writing for:**
- Retail investors
- Day traders
- Developers
- Venture capitalists
- General public

## 4. What We Never Say

| # | Banned Phrase | Why | Say Instead |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "AI-powered" | Implies hype over substance. Our audience distrusts AI marketing. The analysis is quantitative, not magical. | `"motor cuantitativo"` or `"analisis automatizado"` |
| 2 | "disrupting" / "disruptive" | Cooperativa CFOs don't want disruption. They want compliance and reliability. | `"mejorando"` or `"automatizando"` |
| 3 | "platform" (alone) | Too generic. Every SaaS claims to be a "platform." Say what the platform does. | `"plataforma de analisis ALM"` or `"herramienta de reportes ALM"` |
| 4 | "dashboard" (to CFOs) | CFOs don't buy dashboards. They buy reports and compliance. | `"informe ejecutivo"`, `"panel de control"`, or `"portal de informes"` |
| 5 | "seamless" | Meaningless without specifics. What exactly is easy? | Be specific: `"cargue un CSV, reciba un PDF en 24 horas"` |
| 6 | "one-stop-shop" / "one platform" | Overpromises. We do ALM reporting. That is enough. | Name the specific capability: `"informes ALM bilingues con cumplimiento COSSEC"` |
| 7 | "cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art" | Unverifiable claims. Our audience wants proven tools, not experimental ones. | `"probado en produccion"` or `"validado con datos reales"` |
| 8 | "revolutionizing" | Implies the industry needs overthrowing. Our clients are the industry. | `"simplificando"` or `"automatizando el proceso de reportes ALM"` |
| 9 | "data-driven" (alone) | Every company claims this. Without specifying which data and which insight, it is noise. | Name the data: `"basado en su hoja de balance y 12 ratios regulatorios"` |
| 10 | "Submit" (on buttons, in isolation) | Too generic, English-only. | `"Enviar datos"` or action-specific: `"Cargar datos / Upload data"` |

**Additional banned patterns:**
- Exclamation marks in professional copy (use periods)
- Emoji in any client-facing text
- "We're excited to announce" -- just announce it
- "Unlock" or "unleash" -- institutional clients don't "unlock" anything
- "Game-changer" -- say what changed and by how much

## 6. Bilingual Convention

### Cooperativa-facing content (primary audience)

**Pattern:** Spanish heading first, English below in smaller text.

```
<h2>Informes ALM bilingues para cooperativas</h2>
<p class="text-sm text-slate-400">Bilingual ALM reports for cooperativas</p>
```

### Bank-facing or CPA-facing content

**Pattern:** English heading first, Spanish below.

```
<h2>Bilingual ALM Reports for Credit Unions</h2>
<p class="text-sm text-slate-400">Informes ALM bilingues para credit unions</p>
```

### Forms

Both languages visible simultaneously. Labels stacked:

```
<label>Correo institucional / Work email</label>
<input placeholder="maria@cooperativa.com" />
```

### Buttons and CTAs

Bilingual, separated by slash. Spanish first for cooperativa audience:

```
<button>Cargar datos / Upload data</button>
<button>Comenzar piloto / Start pilot</button>
<button>Descargar informe / Download report</button>
```

### Error messages

Always bilingual, single string:

```
"El archivo excede 2MB. / File exceeds 2MB limit."
"Error de conexion. Intente de nuevo. / Network error. Please try again."
```

### Email templates

Full Spanish section first, visual divider (`<hr>`), then full English section. Never interleaved.

### Loading states

Always bilingual:

```
"Procesando... / Processing..."
"Cargando... / Loading..."
"Validando datos... / Validating data..."
```

### Technical terms that stay in English

These terms are used in English even in Spanish context because they are industry-standard:
- ALM (Asset-Liability Management)
- ALCO (Asset-Liability Committee)
- NII (Net Interest Income)
- NIM (Net Interest Margin)
- LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio)
- VaR (Value at Risk)
- SOFR, Fed Funds, Treasury
- CSV, PDF
- Monte Carlo

## 7. Ten Before/After Rewrites

### 1. Pricing page kicker

**BEFORE:**
> Simple pricing for the first wedge

**AFTER:**
> Precios claros para su analisis ALM / Clear pricing for your ALM analysis

**Why:** "First wedge" is internal go-to-market jargon. The customer does not care about your wedge strategy. They care about what they are buying and how much it costs.

---

### 2. Pricing page description

**BEFORE:**
> CERNIQ is being sold first as bilingual ALM reporting software for cooperativas, credit unions, and the advisory firms that support them. Keep the pricing easy to understand and easy to buy.

**AFTER:**
> CERNIQ genera informes ALM bilingues COSSEC-aligned para cooperativas, credit unions y las firmas CPA que las asesoran. Precios transparentes, sin contratos a largo plazo.
>
> CERNIQ generates bilingual COSSEC-aligned ALM reports for cooperativas, credit unions, and the CPA firms that advise them. Transparent pricing, no long-term contracts.

**Why:** The original is an internal sales memo that accidentally shipped to production. It tells the customer how the company thinks about selling, not what the customer is buying. The rewrite speaks to the CFO directly.

---

### 3. Login page tagline

**BEFORE:**
> Intelligence Engineered

**AFTER:**
> Plataforma de Analisis ALM

**Why:** "Intelligence Engineered" sounds like a defense contractor tagline. A cooperativa CFO opening the login page needs to know this is their ALM reporting tool, not a generic analytics product.

---

### 4. Login page description

**BEFORE:**
> Access CERNIQ market data, portfolio risk, valuation, options, execution, and reporting workflows from one secure workspace.

**AFTER:**
> Acceda a sus informes ALM, datos regulatorios y analisis COSSEC desde un portal seguro.
>
> Access your ALM reports, regulatory data, and COSSEC analysis from a secure portal.

**Why:** The original lists six features, most of which (options, execution, valuation) are not relevant to the cooperativa audience. "One secure workspace" is generic SaaS language. The rewrite scopes to what the user actually does: view ALM reports and COSSEC compliance data.

---

### 5. Pricing page strategy section

**BEFORE:**
> Lead with the pilot, not platform complexity.

**AFTER:**
> Comience con un piloto. Escale cuando este listo. / Start with a pilot. Scale when ready.

**Why:** The original is an internal directive to the sales team, not a message for the customer. "Platform complexity" admits the product is complex -- never tell the customer that. The rewrite speaks to the CFO's decision process.

---

### 6. Landing page secondary CTA

**BEFORE:**
> Comenzar -- $750

**AFTER:**
> Comenzar piloto -- $750 / Start pilot -- $750

**Why:** "Comenzar" alone is vague. What are they beginning? The rewrite specifies "piloto" (the exact product being purchased) and adds the English mirror per bilingual button convention.

---

### 7. Win-back email subject

**BEFORE:**
> Le extranamos -- novedades en CERNIQ / We miss you -- CERNIQ updates

**AFTER:**
> Novedades en CERNIQ -- mejoras para su institucion / Updates from CERNIQ -- improvements for your institution

**Why:** "We miss you" is consumer-app language (think Netflix or Spotify). A senior risk analyst does not tell a CFO "I miss you." The rewrite leads with value -- new improvements relevant to their institution.

---

### 8. Dashboard loading state

**BEFORE:**
> Cargando dashboard

**AFTER:**
> Cargando panel de control... / Loading dashboard...

**Why:** "Dashboard" is English inside a Spanish string. For consistency with the brand voice guide, use the Spanish equivalent "panel de control." Add the bilingual convention with English mirror.

---

### 9. Dashboard mixed-language button

**BEFORE:**
> Actualizar Balance Sheet

**AFTER:**
> Actualizar Hoja de Balance / Update Balance Sheet

**Why:** "Actualizar" is Spanish, "Balance Sheet" is English. This half-translated string breaks the bilingual convention. Either go fully Spanish or provide both languages.

---

### 10. Landing page "Plataforma Recurrente" tier name

**BEFORE:**
> Plataforma Recurrente / Recurring Platform

**AFTER:**
> Acceso Recurrente / Recurring Access

**Why:** "Plataforma" used alone is a banned word per Section 4. It is too generic -- every SaaS product calls itself a platform. "Acceso Recurrente" describes what the customer actually gets: ongoing access to the upload-to-report workflow.

## Appendix: Quick Reference Card

**For every piece of copy, ask:**

1. Would a cooperativa CFO understand this in 5 seconds?
2. Is Spanish first (for cooperativa content)?
3. Does it contain at least one mandated phrase?
4. Does it avoid all 10 banned phrases?
5. Is the English mirror present where required?
6. Does it cite a specific number, ratio, or timeline?

If the answer to any of these is "no," rewrite before shipping.
