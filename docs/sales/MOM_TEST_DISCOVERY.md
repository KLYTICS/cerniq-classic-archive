# The "Mom Test" Customer Discovery Guide for CERNIQ

## The Core Principles of The Mom Test
1. **Talk about their life instead of your idea.**
2. **Ask about specifics in the past instead of generics or opinions about the future.**
3. **Talk less and listen more.**

### 🚫 The "Bad" Questions (What NOT to ask)
- "Do you think a tool that automates ALM reporting would be useful?" *(Forces a compliment)*
- "Would your credit union pay $300/month to save 40 hours of work?" *(Hypothetical future)*
- "If we had a feature that translated reports to Spanish, would you use it?" *(Feature hunting)*

---

## 🎯 The Scripts

### Script 1: The Initial Approach (Cold/Warm Intro)
**Goal:** Secure 15 minutes without pitching the product. You are seeking industry expertise, not validation of your app.

> "Hi [Name], I'm researching how financial institutions in PR handle their quarterly regulatory and COSSEC compliance reporting right now. I know you've been working at [Institution] for [X] years and likely oversee this. I'm trying to understand the actual workflow before I dive into solving any bottlenecks. Would you have 15 minutes next week to walk me through how your team pulled together the Q1 ALM report?"

---

### Script 2: The Interview Core (Past Behavior & Current Pain)

#### Openers (Warming up)
- "Walk me through how your team prepared for the most recent COSSEC ALM filing."
- "Who was involved in putting the last ALM report together?"

#### Digging into the Workflow
- "When you got to the duration gap calculation last month, how exactly did that happen? What tools were open on the screen?"
- "Can you show me (or tell me about) the spreadsheets you used for the last cycle?"
- "What was the absolute hardest part of completing the report last quarter?"

#### Validating the Pain
- "You mentioned it took a few weeks. What specifically took the majority of that time?"
- "How much time did your team spend formatting the final document for the board vs. doing the actual math?"
- "When was the last time an error slipped into the board presentation, and how did you guys catch it?"

#### Checking for Existing Solutions (Willingness to Pay)
- "Have you tried to speed this process up in the past?"
- *(If yes)* "What did you try? Why didn't it stick?"
- *(If no)* "Why haven't you looked for a different way to do it?" *(This usually reveals if it's a pain they actually care about solving or if they just accept it as part of the job)*
- "How much are you currently paying your external advisors/consultants to help with risk models right now?"

#### Bilingual / Board Dynamics (The Cerniq "Wedge")
- "At the last board meeting, how did you present the ALM findings? English, Spanish, or both?"
- "Who handles translating the technical financial data into Spanish for the COSSEC filing?"
- "Can you recall a specific time the board asked a question about a rate shock scenario? How were you able to answer them with the current setup?"

---

### 🛑 Handling "The Pitch"
In a Mom Test interview, if the prospect says, *"Are you guys building something for this?"*
Don't jump immediately into a full product demo. Keep the focus on them.

**Response:**
> "Yes, actually. We noticed that every cooperativa seems to spend 40+ hours manually pasting Excel charts into Word docs for the board and COSSEC. We're building something to make that happen in 60 seconds. But honestly, hearing how you guys handle the [specific problem they just mentioned] is super helpful to make sure we aren't building the wrong thing. Based on what you just told me about your translation process, does that timeframe (60 seconds) sound like it actually matters to you, or is the current process 'good enough'?"

---

## 📝 5 Golden Rules for Cerniq Interviews
1. **Never say "ALM software" until they do.** Call it "the quarterly reporting process" or "COSSEC compliance prep."
2. **If they say "we use Excel," ask to see it.** Seeing their massive, tangled 50-tab spreadsheet is your greatest validation.
3. **Listen for the emotional words.** "Frustrating", "time-consuming", "tedious", "stressful". Write these down. Use these *exact* words in your cold emails and marketing copy.
4. **Follow the money.** Ask who signs off on the tool budget and what software they *did* buy recently.
5. **Get a commitment.** At the end, ask for a commitment, not a compliment. (e.g., "If I had a prototype tomorrow that generated the report fully in Spanish, would you be willing to give me a dummy data set to run it on?")
