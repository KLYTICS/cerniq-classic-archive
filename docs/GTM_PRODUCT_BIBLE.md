- Tier 4 (<$50M): 24 cooperativas, $0.1B assets

### 1.2 The Regulatory Tailwind

**COSSEC Tightening Standards (Post-2020):**
COSSEC has significantly raised ALM examination standards following the 2008 financial crisis lessons and the rising-rate environment of 2022-2024. Specific findings:
- Interest Rate Risk (IRR) examination deficiency: 60%+ of cooperativas received COSSEC examination findings related to inadequate IRR models or asset-liability management documentation
- Many cooperativas lack formalized ALM policies, stress-testing frameworks, or duration gap analysis
- COSSEC examinations now require: documented ALM policy, quarterly board-reviewed metrics (NII sensitivity, EVE, duration gap), stress-testing scenarios, and rate scenario documentation

**Rising Rate Environment Exposure:**
Cooperativas with asset-heavy, liability-sensitive balance sheets were hit hard in 2022-2024 as deposit rates rose and loan prepayments accelerated. Those without proper ALM models didn't see it coming. CERNIQ directly addresses this gap.

**NCUA Convergence:**
PR credit unions under NCUA regulation face similar IRR examination standards. CERNIQ's bilingual approach and cooperativa expertise positions it for this market as well.

### 1.3 ALM Spending Patterns

**Current State:**
- Status quo: 80% of cooperativas use Excel + manual consultant engagement
- Average annual ALM consulting spend: $15K-$40K per cooperativa (varies by size)
- Average consulting turnaround: 2-4 weeks for quarterly ALM report
- Typical engagement: CPA firm or independent consultant runs 4 reports/year

**Total Addressable Market (TAM) Calculation:**
- 109 cooperativas × $25K average annual ALM spend = **$2.725B TAM (highest bound)**
- More conservative: 109 × $15K = **$1.635B TAM (lower bound)**
- Realistic estimate: **$2.0B TAM for PR-only cooperativa ALM services/software**

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM) for CERNIQ:**
- Realistic TAM for cooperativa-specific ALM software (not all consulting): $300M-$500M
- CERNIQ's realistic SAM (software + embedded consulting): **$15M-$40M in PR+US Hispanic credit unions**
- First 18-month focus: **$3M-$8M in PR cooperativas alone** (top 50 institutions)

### 1.4 The CPA Firm Channel Opportunity

**Current Dynamics:**
- 15-20 CPA firms in PR serve the cooperativa audit/compliance market (ICPA directory)
- Most CPA firms do ALM manually: they build Excel models, run scenarios, write board reports

**Cooperativa size: $100M-$500M assets (Tier 2)**

- Technology obsolescence: their core system's ALM module is weak

- NII (Net Interest Income) at risk under various rate scenarios
